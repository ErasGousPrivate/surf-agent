# Muizenberg, Cemetery & Glencairn Surf Conditions Guide

Beginner-friendly beta sheet for Cape Town's False Bay surf spots. Compiled
from Surfline, Surf-Forecast.com, WannaSurf, Surfnerd, and other public surf
guides (July 2026). Numeric wind-speed thresholds are general rules of
thumb, not spot-verified figures — treat as approximate.

## Jargon

- **Swell**: organized waves from a distant storm, described by height and
  period (seconds between waves — bigger number = more powerful, better
  organized).
- **Swell direction**: compass direction the swell is coming FROM.
- **Ground swell vs wind swell**: ground swell = long-period, clean,
  powerful, distant storm. Wind swell = short-period, choppy, local wind.
- **Onshore wind**: sea → land. Messy, crumbly, bad for surfing.
- **Offshore wind**: land → sea. Holds the wave face up, smoother, good.
- **Cross-shore wind**: roughly parallel to the beach. Middling.
- **Beach break**: wave breaks over sand (vs. reef/point). Banks shift
  week to week. Generally more forgiving to fall on than reef.
- **Close-out**: wave breaks all at once along its length — nothing to
  ride, just whitewater.
- **Cape Doctor**: Cape Town's strong, persistent summer south-easterly wind.

## Why False Bay behaves differently

Cape Town's Atlantic (western) side faces the open ocean and takes big
SW Atlantic ground swells directly. False Bay — where Muizenberg,
Cemetery, and Glencairn all sit — is tucked behind Cape Point on the
eastern side. Swell has to bend/wrap around the Cape to get in, which
strips out size and power. That's why False Bay is the gentler,
beginner-friendly side of the city, especially when the Atlantic side is
too big or blown out.

## Muizenberg ("Surfer's Corner")

Cape Town's classic learner wave — a wide, sandy beach break in the corner
of False Bay, famous for longboarders and mellow rolling waves.

**Best conditions:**
- Swell size: small to moderate, ~1-4 ft (0.3-1.2 m) face. Doesn't need
  much swell to work.
- Swell direction: wrapped SW ground swell is the main winter engine,
  topped up by smaller local S/SE swell. Arrives softened and gentle.
- Wind: light, or offshore. NW is the single most consistently cited
  offshore direction (some sources broaden to NW-N). Light wind of any
  direction is generally fine — the bay is somewhat sheltered here.
- Tide: works across most of the cycle, but classic long, peeling,
  longboard-friendly walls happen most reliably around low tide when
  it's small and clean. As swell size picks up, a bit more tide (toward
  mid) keeps things manageable; high tide is really only better once it's
  genuinely big and you want a punchier, shortboard-style inside wave.
- Season: classic conditions cluster April-September, especially
  June-August, when wind flips NW and small wrapping swells sneak in.

**Acceptable/marginal:** 4-6 ft swell still rideable but overpowers the
gentle character. Moderate cross-shore or light onshore wind is textured
but surfable, especially early morning. Under 1 ft is often too flat.

**Avoid:** Strong SE ("Cape Doctor") turns onshore/cross-shore here and
chops the surface — Muizenberg is often blown out on strong SE days.
Swell over ~6 ft face closes the beach out and currents get strong. Very
strong onshore wind from any southerly quadrant.

## Cemetery

A quieter beach break further east along the same stretch of sand as
Muizenberg, off Baden Powell Drive. Similar sandy, forgiving character to
Muizenberg but with far less infrastructure — no surf schools, no
permanent lifeguards. Suits a self-sufficient beginner or early
intermediate more than a first-ever lesson.

**Best conditions:**
- Swell size: works across a range — no strong seasonal signature the way
  Muizenberg has. Only really fires when wind, swell direction, and tide
  all line up together.
- Swell direction: SSE-SE ground swell is ideal — more directly southerly
  than Muizenberg's SW-dominant pattern.
- Wind: offshore here is NW, same as Muizenberg. Cemetery is a more open,
  exposed stretch without Muizenberg's bay-corner shelter — wind
  direction and strength both matter more, and it degrades faster in bad
  wind than Muizenberg does.
- Tide: best around low tide.
- Season: no single dominant season, but autumn — especially April — has
  the best long-term stats.
- Character: sandy beach break, peaks peel both left and right; noticeably
  quieter than Muizenberg most days.

**Acceptable/marginal:** Moderate cross-shore or light onshore wind is
still rideable, just less clean, and degrades faster here than at
Muizenberg. Small swell can still be fun on a longer board.

**Avoid:** Strong onshore or cross-shore wind — fully exposed with no bay
shelter, turns to mush faster than Muizenberg or Glencairn in the same
wind. Big swell gets heavy and disorganized quickly without the bay's
natural protection.

## Glencairn (Beach — the spot configured in this project)

A sandy beach break generally regarded as the next step up from
Muizenberg once you're comfortable there and want a bit more push.

**Best conditions:**
- Swell size: small to moderate, ~1.5-4 ft — similar window to Muizenberg.
- Swell direction: a SE swell reaching the bay fairly directly is the
  headline ideal here — more so than at Muizenberg, where wrapped SW
  groundswell does more of the work. Also picks up wrapped SW/S swell.
- Wind: best on WNW-NW, offshore — same sheltered-from-the-Cape-Doctor
  logic as Muizenberg, though being more open to the bay it can still
  catch some wind texture.
- Tide: best around low tide, when the bank creates a punchier,
  better-defined wave; can go soft and mushy at high tide.
- Season: fairly consistent year-round, but still leans toward the same
  April-September window as Muizenberg for the cleanest days.

**Acceptable/marginal:** Light-to-moderate cross-shore wind is tolerable
but degrades wave quality faster here than at Muizenberg, since Glencairn
isn't as tucked into the bay's shelter. Mid-tide is fine but generally
less punchy/consistent than low tide.

**Avoid:** Strong SE wind — same story as Muizenberg, turns
onshore/cross-shore and chops the surface. Strong rip currents are
specifically flagged for this spot — take extra care. Big swell days
generally make this stretch of coast feel heavier and busier.

## Side-by-side comparison

| Criteria | Muizenberg | Cemetery | Glencairn |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skill level suited | True beginners, longboarders | Self-sufficient beginner/early intermediate | Beginner-intermediate, step up from Muizenberg |
| Wave character | Soft, rolling, forgiving | Similar forgiving sandy break, quieter | Punchier, more shape |
| Ideal swell size | 1-4 ft | Variable, no strong seasonal signature | 1.5-4 ft |
| Ideal swell direction | Wrapped SW + local S/SE | SSE-SE groundswell | SE most directly; also SW/S |
| Ideal wind | Light or NW (offshore) | Light or NW (offshore) | Light or WNW-NW (offshore) |
| Ideal tide | Low-to-mid; more tide once bigger | Low | Low |
| Worst wind | Strong SE | Any strong onshore/cross-shore | Strong SE |
| Crowds | Busiest | Quietest of the three | Quieter, fewer schools |
| When to pick this spot | Default for learners, or small/clean swell | Muizenberg too crowded, comfortable going it alone | Muizenberg too soft/flat/crowded, want more push |

## Quick decision order

1. **Wind first.** Light or offshore (NW/WNW at all three)? Good. Strong
   SE? Skip all three, or go very early before it fills in (SE usually
   builds through the day).
2. **Swell size.** Under ~4 ft face at Muizenberg/Glencairn = comfortable
   learner-to-intermediate range. Cemetery is less about size, more about
   everything lining up at once. Over ~4-6 ft = experienced surfers only.
3. **Tide.** Muizenberg: low-to-mid, especially when small. Cemetery and
   Glencairn: low. Wind and swell direction still matter more than tide
   at any of the three.

**Rule of thumb:** if you can only check one thing, check the wind. A
small, "wrong-direction" swell in light offshore wind will almost always
be more fun than a good swell ruined by strong onshore chop.
